3. Crowdsourcing Platforms

Places you can post crowdsourcing tasks

In this section, we show you how to set up accounts and post tasks on some of the Internet’s most popular crowdsourcing platforms. We also discuss the pros and cons of each platform. No service has the same crowd. Workers have different skillsets, are based in different geographic locations, and ask for different levels of pay. Nor is the hiring process consistent between platforms. Some merely require you to post the task and wait for workers to show up, while others require you to interview and hire workers. This variation will be discussed in greater detail below.

1. Amazon Mechanical Turk

Amazon Mechanical Turk, commonly referred to by those in the know as “Turk” or “MTurk,” contains a built-in task designer that enables you to create a task on the web and populate it with data. Turk requires you to make a number of design choices, including restricting the country of origin for your workers, their price per job, and the amount of time they may spend on each task. This is not an exhaustive list of the options Turk provides.

1.1 Getting set up

  1. Sign in with an Amazon.com account
  2. Buy some credits
  3. Click “Create a HIT”

1.2 Why MTurk?

Mechanical Turk is widely used by academics.

1.3 Why not MTurk?

Turk requires substantial experimentation to get things right. I One limitation to Turk is that Amazon now restricts worker accounts to the United States and India. If your task requires workers from outside of those two countries, Turk is likely inappropriate.

2. oDesk

oDesk’s worker selection process is unique. After an application/interview process, it requires you to hire individual crowd workers. This is more time consuming than MTurk, but oDesk workers’ skills may be more directly relevant to your project. 2.1 Getting set up
  1. Sign up for an oDesk account
  2. Link a credit card for payments
  3. Click “post a job”

2.2 Why oDesk?

oDesk provides lots of options for searching for workers. Options include prices, skills, and geographic locations. As a starting point, we provide a basic procedure for worker selection that you can use in the hiring process. In addition, you are able to communicate directly with the workers you select. They may ask you questions about the nature of the work. We also include a set of standard responses to these questions.

2.3 Why not oDesk?

oDesk requires you to manually identify and select individual workers for your project.

3. MobileWorks

MobileWorks manages their own crowd so that requesters do not have to manage workers, run interviews, or implement quality control.

3.1 Getting set up

  1. Sign up for a MobileWorks account
  2. Purchase an hourly plan (“personal”)
  3. Click “post a project”

3.2 Why MobileWorks?

MobileWorks fixes many design choices. Although it is not required, you may communicate directly with assigned workers. In order to help you generate results with little variance, we provide you with standard task templates for benchmarking.
